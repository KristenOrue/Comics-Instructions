How to Choose a Comic Book

Introduction:

My name is Kristen Orue and I LOVE Comic books. The comic book live action movie is now mainstream and with the success of multiple comic book stories, comes great responsibility. Most of my friends do not read comic books, but whenever we go to a comic book movie they ask: “How does it compare to the comic book?” followed by “Will you recommend some for me?” I created this site as guide to anyone who has ever asked these questions and wants to find some answers. I hope you eventually love comics as much as I do. Let the search Begin!

What you'll need:

• Computer/phone with web access
• Local Comic Book store and/or local library
• Pen and paper

1. Choose your Genre

There are a variety of genres within the Comic Book world just like any other art form. There are stories of Mystery, Horror, fantasy, Adventure, Romance, and some with a combination of a few. Write down a list of some of your favorite book genres.

Variety of Graphic Novels

2. Click on the sites provided in the Navigation Bar

There are a variety of Comic Book Publishers. Marvel and DC Comics are the most recognisable, but there is also a variety of publishers which are independently owned and tend to release artist's passion projects such as Image Comics, Dark Horse Comics, and BOOM Studios. Overall, the stories released by the independently owned publishers have more diversity. Artists and writers can go in whatever direction they want which can include a serial killer who's fetish is to bite people's nails into their fingers, a video game character from a Horror themed game accidently ends up in fairyland and has to adjust, to very real stories about political termoil in the past referencing today's political climate or a Muslim woman who is being discriminated against and is also haunted by a demon. The variety of the outlandish to very real human stories is out there just waiting to be read and enjoyed by you, whatever your taste may be.

I provided links to the the main Comic Book publishers. Click around and do your research.

3. Explore!

These sites are where the publishers showcase new releases and old books that the publisher feel are still worth reading. For example, when I started reading comics, I started from a new storyline and missed all of the beautifully written and drawn comics of the old days which were recommended to me later on so simply write down what you think is ascetically appealing.

4. Take Notes

Take a note either on your phone or on a piece of paper and write down which characters or artwork stands out to you. Everyone is different (Thank goodness!) in their taste and this will help narrow down yours. Write down the artists or the author if the storyline sounds like something you’d be interested in.

5. Find your Local Comic Shop

Click on the image below to find your local Comic book shop…

NOTE: Wednesdays is New comic book day so if you want time to discuss your selections, receive feedback, and perhaps other recommendations from your friendly neighborhood comic book employee DO NOT GO ON A WEDNESDAY. It is extremely busy. Comic books with an active storyline are released monthly just like magazine subscriptions and it could be any one of those Wednesdays.

6. Talk to a Human

Once you arrive at your local Comic Book shop talk to the person working there. Mention that you’re new to comics and provide him/her with the list of comics you’ve already shown an interest in. They will be able to guide you in the right direction from there.

If you walk in empty handed they could be a bit biased based on their own taste

However, show them a list and they'll show you more like it and get you started with a trade paperback edition instead of a storyline that you may have to work on catching up to enjoy.

7. Check your library

If you are only interested in those comic books that you selected, check your local library first they usually have quite the variety and you’ll be surprised by what they have to offer. Here in Bend, OR search the website…

8. Pick what YOU like

An important rule of picking your very first comic book story: DO NOT pick a comic book because you think it will be worth something someday. The comic books that are worth something one day are the ones that nobody imagines will be worth something one day. Just take good care of whatever storyline you find interesting.

9. HAVE FUN!

Most importantly, HAVE FUN! Appreciate the fact that a writer had a story to tell alongside a vision in mind handed it over to an artist and allowed them to completely take over the story through visualization. It’s a fun and beautiful relationship. Buy one book and if you like it, Buy more. If you don't like it, don't buy more and pick something else.
In conclusion, this is exciting for people like me who already love comics but, I certainly have my fair share of comics I didn't enjoy no different than some novels that sounded great and were simply underwhelming. I hope this set of instructions helps and that you've had fun!
